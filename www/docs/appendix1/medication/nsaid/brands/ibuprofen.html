Ibuprofen

Back

Brand names: Advil, Motrin

Adult Starting Dose (Max Per Day)

  • 400mg to 600mg 3 to 4 times daily for HA in the mTBI patient
  • For episodic HA, use prn at HA onset up to 3 days per week

Advantages

  • Take with food or milk to minimize GI upset
  • When using combination products, do not exceed maximum recommended daily doses of ibuprofen
  • Maximum of 3200mg/day

Disadvantages

  • Contraindications: include hypersensitivity to aspirin or other NSAIDs. Do not give to one who has had symptoms from other NSAIDs of asthma, rhinitis, urticaria, nasal polyps, bronchospasm or other symptoms of allergic or anaphylactic reactions
  • Adverse Events: GI upset, GI bleed, dizziness, vertigo
  • Potential renal impairment with long term use
  • May cause hyperkalemia
  • Stevens-Johnson syndrome may occur. Discontinue use at first sign of rash
  • Avoid use in severe hepatic impairment

Pregnancy Category

  • Controlled studies show no risk
  • Positive evidence of risk; However potential benefits may outweigh potential risks (3rd trimester)

Safety Margins

  • If patients exhibit gastrointestinal side effects with NSAIDs, therapy with proton-pump inhibitors and histamine blockers may be considered
  • Avoid concomitant use with alcohol
  • Avoid concomitant use of NSAID with salicylates
  • If using warfarin with NSAIDs, monitor PT closely

Efficacy

  • NSAIDs are effective medications for pain relief for patients with mTBI
  • Ibuprofen liquid filled gel caps are FDA approved to treat migraines
