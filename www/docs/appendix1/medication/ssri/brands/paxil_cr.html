Paroxetine CR

Back

Brand name: Paxil CR

Adult Starting Dose (Max Per Day)

  • Initial adult dose = 25mg QD
  • Max adult dose/day = 62.5mg QD
  • Initial geriatric dose = 12.5mg QD
  • Max geriatric dose = 50mg daily QD

Advantages

  • May be taken with or without food.
  • AM daily dosing
  • Do not crush or chew CR tab
  • Generic

Disadvantages

  • Of the SSRIs, highest reported rate of discontinuation syndrome, highest rate of sexual dysfunction and weight gain
  • Sometimes sedating and more anti-cholinergic symptoms
  • Possibly more CYP450 interactions

Pregnancy Category

  • Positive evidence of risk; However potential benefits may outweigh potential risks

Safety Margin for SSRIs

  • Wide margin of safety, rare deaths reported on over dose
  • Contraindicated with pimozide or with MAOIs within two weeks of discontinuing MAOIs
  • Fluoxetine or paroxetine contraindicated with thioridazine within five weeks of discontinuing therapy
  • Contraindicated sertraline oral concentrate with disulfiram
  • Do not take citalopram and escitalopram concomitantly
  • Do not initiate concomitant therapy with a benzodiazepine
  • Drug interactions may include Tricyclic Antidepressants, NSAIDs, SNRIs, Triptans, aspirin, carbamazepine, warfarin, nilotinib, sibutramine, tamoxifen, tetrabenazine and ziprasidone
  • Avoid concomitant use with alcohol, l-tryptophan and st. john's wort
  • Taper dose slowly to prevent clinically significant discontinuation symptoms

Efficacy for SSRIs

  • Depression: Response rate: mild depression = 4-8 weeks; Moderate depression = 8-12 week; Severe depression = augmentative strategies, concurrent administration of adjuncts (referral to specialist)
  • Recommended as monotherapy as first-line therapy in PTSD
  • PTSD: Paroxetine IR and sertraline approved for use in PTSD; Efficacy for long-term use in PTSD has not been systematically evaluated
  • Escitalopram and fluoxetine have good documentation for off-label use in PTSD
