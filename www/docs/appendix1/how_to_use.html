Purpose

The purpose of this guide is to assist the primary care provider in using the medication tables. This guide does not replace clinical judgment or specialty consultation.

How to Use this Guide

  1. Identify the medication to be use based on the target symptoms (depression, substance use disorder, chronic pain, PTSD, sleep disorders, stimulation, smoking cessation and nightmares).
  2. Refer to the specific page for the information on the medication desired. See below for specific guidance on each field.
  3. Each medication page includes information on the specific medications. Reference this table for information on dosing, advantages, disadvantages, pregnancy risk, safety, efficacy, monitoring, referrals and warning.
  4. The Adverse Effects section includes information on specific adverse effects with relative comparisons of the medications in a specific class.
  5. The Pros and Cons section contains information on the pros and cons as it relates to using these medications for the co-occurring disorders.

Fields

Information in this section includes caution statements and general information pertaining to all the medications in each section.

Generic (Brand Name)

This field includes the generic name first and then the brand name in parenthesis. There may be more than one brand name listed.

Adult Starting Dose (Max Per Day)

The doses are listed in this field. The initial starting dose is listed, titration information if available and the maximum dose per day. Adult and geriatric doses are listed.

Advantages

Advantages pertaining to the specific medication are listed in this field. Information in this section may include FDA approved indications, co-occurring disorder indications and specific dosage instructions.

Disadvantages

Disadvantages pertaining to the specific medication are listed in this field. Information in this section includes contraindications related to diseases, adverse side effects and common side effects.

Pregnancy Categories

The specific pregnancy categories are listed here and are either A, B, C or D.

Pregnancy categories

Safety Margin

Information in this section includes instructions on toxicity, overdose, contraindications with other medications, concomitant use with other medications and tapering information.

Efficacy

Included in this section is the medication efficacy as it relates to the co-occurring disorders.

Black Box Warning

Monitoring, Referrals and Warnings: Monitoring, referral and warning instructions are listed in this section.

Black Box Warning: Black box warnings or box warnings are listed in this section. These warnings appear on a prescription drug's label and are designed to call attention to serious or life-threatening risks.
